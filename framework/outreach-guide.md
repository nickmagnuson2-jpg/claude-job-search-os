# Outreach Guide

Durable reference for all outreach skills (`/cold-outreach`, `/follow-up`, `/draft-email`). Read this before drafting any message.

---

## Email Frameworks (ranked for CoS / strategic ops)

### 1. Persona-Based
Shared identity — alumni, same industry, mutual connections. Highest trust and response rate.

**When to use:** Shared school, former employer, mutual contact, same professional community.

**Structure:**
1. Identity hook ("Fellow Tuck alum here…")
2. Brief credibility line (impact, not title)
3. Specific connection to their work
4. Low-pressure ask

### 2. 3Ps (Praise-Picture-Push)
Warm outreach to people whose work you genuinely admire.

**When to use:** You've followed their work, read their content, or respect a specific decision they made.

**Structure:**
1. **Praise** — specific, earned compliment (reference something real)
2. **Picture** — paint a picture of what you'd love to learn or discuss
3. **Push** — gentle CTA (coffee, 15-min call)

### 3. PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
Cold outreach naming a specific operational pain you can address.

**When to use:** You've identified a concrete problem the company faces that maps to your experience.

**Structure:**
1. **Problem** — name the challenge (1 sentence)
2. **Agitate** — why it matters now (growth stage, market pressure, team scaling)
3. **Solve** — position yourself as someone who's navigated this before

### 4. BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
Positioning as transformation agent at inflection-point companies.

**When to use:** Company at a clear inflection point — post-funding, pre-launch, scaling, pivoting.

**Structure:**
1. **Before** — where the company is now (acknowledge the moment)
2. **After** — what success looks like at the next stage
3. **Bridge** — how your experience connects the two

### 5. AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
Structured cold outreach with clear value proposition. Generic fallback.

**When to use:** No strong personal connection, but a clear value prop to communicate.

**Structure:**
1. **Attention** — hook with a relevant insight or shared context
2. **Interest** — why this matters to them specifically
3. **Desire** — what you bring (impact, not credentials)
4. **Action** — single clear CTA

## Waterfall Personalization Logic

Pick the best available signal, in priority order:

1. **Shared identity** — alumni, mutual contact, same company history
2. **Recent trigger event** — funding round, product launch, promotion, public post
3. **Tech stack or domain overlap** — you've worked with their tools or in their space
4. **Industry pain point** — a known challenge in their sector you can address
5. **Role-specific value prop** — generic fallback based on job description

Always use the highest-priority signal available. If signal #1 exists, lead with it even if #2 is also present — mention #2 as a secondary hook.

## The Three-Question Quality Gate

Every draft must pass before output:

1. **"Why you?"** — Is there a specific reason for reaching out to *this person* (not just anyone at the company)?
2. **"Why now?"** — Is there a trigger event or timing factor making this timely?
3. **"Why me?"** — Does the sender establish credibility for this conversation?

If any answer is weak, revise the draft before presenting. A draft with all three strong signals has 3–5x higher response rates than generic outreach.

## Subject Line Rules

- **2–4 words** optimal (46% open rate vs. 34% at 10 words)
- **Questions** perform best (46% open rate)
- **Personalized with name**: +31% over generic
- **Lowercase** slightly outperforms title case in cold outreach
- **Never:** ALL CAPS, urgency words ("URGENT", "Time-sensitive"), clickbait, "Touching base", "Quick question", "Following up" (save for actual follow-ups)

Examples:
- `Tuck alum — coffee?`
- `[Name], quick thought`
- `Re: [specific topic]`
- `[Mutual contact] suggested I reach out`

### LinkedIn Connect — Real Example (Persona-Based, shared employer)

> **Context:** Ella Brown, Chief of Staff at Odyssey PBC. Both Nick and Ella worked at Altman Solon at different times; no personal overlap. Sent 2026-02-25.

```
Hi Ella, I came across Odyssey while exploring the psychedelic therapy space
and noticed we both did time at Altman Solon. Would love to connect and hear
how you ended up there.
```

**Why it works:** Leads with the shared-employer hook without overstating familiarity ("did time at" rather than "we worked together"). Company-specific research signal ("psychedelic therapy space") shows genuine interest, not spray-and-pray. Single soft ask — hear *her* story, not a pitch about Nick. 172 chars.

## Optimal Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Word count | 75–125 words | 52% booking rate vs. 20% at 300+ words |
| Send timing | Tue–Thu, 9–11 AM or 2–4 PM recipient TZ | Highest open rates in mid-morning, mid-afternoon |
| Follow-up cadence | 3–5 touches, 3–5 business day spacing | Diminishing returns after 5 |
| Thank-you timing | Within 2–24 hours | 80% of hiring managers say it influences decisions |
| Reading level | 6th grade | Shorter sentences, simpler words, higher comprehension |
| CTAs per email | Exactly 1 | Multiple CTAs reduce response rate by 30%+ |
| Paragraphs | 2–4 short paragraphs | Wall of text kills response rates |

## Follow-Up Sequence

Research shows 3–5 follow-ups spaced 3–5 business days apart are optimal.

| Follow-up # | Timing | Approach |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| 1st | 2–3 days after initial | Gentle nudge, reference original, add one new insight |
| 2nd | 5–8 days after initial | New angle — share relevant article, company news, or question |
| 3rd | 10–15 days after initial | Brief, direct — "wanted to bump this up" + clear ask |
| 4th | 20–28 days after initial | Last attempt — "I know you're busy, one last note" |
| 5th+ | 35+ days | Break-up: "No pressure, just wanted to leave the door open" |

**Critical rule:** Each follow-up MUST add new value — never just "checking in" or "circling back." Check `output/<slug>/` and web for recent developments to reference.

## Anti-Patterns (banned in all drafts)

### Filler Openers
- "I hope this finds you well"
- "I'm reaching out because"
- "I hope you're having a great week"
- "I wanted to touch base"

### Hedge Language
- "I was wondering if perhaps you might consider"
- "I don't want to take up too much of your time, but"
- "I know you're really busy"
- "Sorry to bother you"

### Structural Problems
- Essay structure: long context before the point
- Multiple CTAs in one message
- Attaching resume unsolicited in cold outreach
- Walls of text with no paragraph breaks

### Content Problems
- Generic flattery: "I've been really impressed by your work" (without specifics)
- Leading with credentials instead of impact
- Premature intimacy with senior executives
- "Pick your brain" / "Touching base" / "Checking in"
- Volunteering negatives ("I don't have experience in X, but…")
- Over-explaining your situation before making the ask

### Tone Problems
- Supplicant tone ("I would be so grateful if you could spare even 5 minutes")
- Overly casual with strangers ("Hey! So I saw your post and…")
- Transactional framing ("I need something from you")

---

## Tone Matching Protocol

1. Read 2–3 of the sender's actual sent messages from `data/networking.md`
2. Analyze: sentence length, formality level, use of contractions, opening/closing patterns, characteristic phrases, emoji usage
3. Draft in that style — few-shot examples beat style descriptions
4. If no prior messages exist, default to: **professional, concise, peer-to-peer** (not supplicant)

Tone calibration by relationship type:
- **Recruiter:** professional, direct, structured
- **Peer / alumni:** warm, conversational, authentic
- **Senior executive:** concise, respectful, specific value-add
- **Mentor / referral:** grateful but not fawning, specific ask

## Forwardable Email Technique

For intro requests, write a self-contained blurb the connector can forward unchanged:

**Structure:**
1. Brief ask to the connector (2–3 sentences)
2. Line break
3. Forwardable blurb (under 100 words):
   - Who you are (1 sentence)
   - Why this company/person specifically (1 sentence)
   - What you're looking for (1 sentence)
   - LinkedIn URL
   - Clear but low-pressure ask

**Use double opt-in language:** "Would you be open to checking if [target] would welcome an intro?" — never assume the connector will forward without asking.
